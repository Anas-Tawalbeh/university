Number of credits taken throughout the course

For master's degree holders, Type 1.1 and Type 2.1, no less than 48 credits.

For those who graduated with a bachelor's degree, Type 1.2 and Type 2.2, no less than 72 credits.

Course format

  • Type

    • It is a doctoral degree program, a 3-year course.

  • Language used

    • Thai, Arabic, Malay and English. For writing a thesis, Students can write their thesis in Thai, Arabic, Malay or English.

  • Admission

    • Both Thai and International students are accepted.

  • Cooperation with other institutions

    • It is a specific course of the institute. that manage teaching directly.

  • Awarding degrees to graduates

    • Give a degree in only one field of study.

Course Operation

  • Date - time of instruction

    • Semester 1: June - September
    • Semester 2: November - March
    • Summer semester: April - May

  • Qualifications of students

    • 2.2.1 Graduated with a bachelor's degree from an educational institution both domestically and internationally recognized by the Office of the Civil Service Commission (OCSC).
    • 2.2.1.1 Graduated with a bachelor's degree from institutions of higher education recognized by the Ministry of Education with the approval of the curriculum administration committee and receive a cumulative grade point average of not less than 3.50 or
    • 2.2.1.2 who are studying in the last semester of bachelor's degree from institutions of higher education recognized by the Ministry of Education with the approval of the curriculum administration committee and received a cumulative grade point average of not less than 3.50 in the last semester or
    • 2.2.1.3 Graduated with a bachelor's degree or equivalent from institutions of higher education recognized by the Ministry of Education and have studied courses in the chosen field of not less than 12 credits and have at least 5 years of experience in the selected field, with the approval of the Program Administrative Committee.
    • 2.2.2 Hold a master's degree from an educational institution both domestically and internationally recognized by the Office of the Civil Service Commission (OCSC).
    • 2.2.2.1 Be a master's degree holder from institutions of higher education recognized by the Ministry of Education with the approval of the curriculum administration committee and receive a cumulative grade point average of not less than 3.25 or
    • 2.2.2.2 Students who are currently studying in the last semester at the master's degree level. from institutions of higher education recognized by the Ministry of Education with the approval of the curriculum administration committee and received a cumulative grade point average of not less than 3.25 in the last semester or
    • 2.2.2.3 Be a master's degree holder. or equivalent from institutions of higher education recognized by the Ministry of Education and have at least 3 years of experience in the chosen field, with the approval of the Program Administrative Committee
    • 2.2.3 Other qualifications apart from those specified are at the discretion of the Program Administrative Committee.
    • 2.2.4 Being a person with good behavior There is no degradation or defect in good morals.
    • 2.2.5 Being physically strong It is not a serious contagious disease or a disease that is an obstacle to study. by certified doctor
    • 2.2.6 Not being a person who has been sentenced to imprisonment Except for an offense committed through negligence or a misdemeanor. or at least 2 certified persons
    • 2.2.7 have English proficiency test results according to the criteria set by the Commission on Higher Education

Occupations that can be engaged after graduation

  • Lecturer/Academician in Islamic Studies
  • Researcher and advanced researcher in Islamic studies according to public and private organizations or institutions
  • Consultant on Islamic studies for public and private organizations or institutions
  • Senior Executives in Islamic Studies of Public and Private Organizations or Institutions
  • Self-employed, such as an academician in Islamic studies Critics of Islamic studies, etc.
